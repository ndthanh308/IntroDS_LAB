\begin{abstract}
 Let $A$, $B$, and $C$ be three $n\times n$ matrices. We investigate the problem of verifying whether $AB=C$ over the ring of integers and finding the correct product $AB$. Given that $C$ is different from $AB$ by at most $k$ entries, we propose an algorithm that uses $O(\sqrt{k}n^2+k^2n)$ operations. 
    %
    Let $\alpha$ be the largest integers in $A$, $B$, and $C$. The largest value involved in the computation is of $O(n^3\alpha^2)$.
\end{abstract}
